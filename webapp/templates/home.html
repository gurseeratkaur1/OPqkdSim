Welcome to the Quantum Key Distribution Simulator

Explore Quantum Cryptography

Quantum Key Distribution (QKD) is a cryptographic protocol that uses the principles of quantum mechanics to establish a secure communication key between two parties.

What can you do with our simulator?
  • Simulate the BB84 protocol, one of the most well-known QKD protocols
  • Adjust parameters like mean photon number, channel distance, and detector properties
  • Visualize key performance metrics including Quantum Bit Error Rate (QBER) and Secret Key Rate (SKR)
  • Explore how different parameters affect the security and efficiency of quantum key distribution
  • Determine optimal settings for secure communication
Key Features:

Our simulator provides several interactive visualizations:

  • QBER vs Mean Photon Number (μ)
  • Secret Key Rate (SKR) vs Mean Photon Number (μ)
  • QBER vs Distance
  • SKR vs Distance

Whether you're a student, researcher, or simply interested in quantum cryptography, our simulator offers an accessible way to explore the fascinating world of quantum key distribution.
